# Code of Conduct

The Huella Latam project has adopted the **Contributor Covenant
version 2.1** as its community Code of Conduct. The full text of the
covenant is maintained at:

- <https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/>

By participating in this project — as a contributor, maintainer,
country implementer, reporter, or user of any community channel — you
agree to abide by the Contributor Covenant 2.1.

The sections that follow customise enforcement and reporting for this
project. Where they overlap with the Contributor Covenant text, the
Covenant text prevails as the substantive standard.

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in Huella Latam a harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or
invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity
and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, colour, religion or
sexual identity and orientation. We pledge to act and interact in
ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive and
healthy community.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces directly
operated by the project — the GitHub repository, GitHub Discussions,
issue and pull-request threads, project events, and any official
communication channel established by the maintainer team. It also
applies when an individual is officially representing the project in
public spaces.

Country deployments operated by adopting governments may publish
additional rules consistent with their local context; those rules
operate in addition to, not in replacement of, this Code of Conduct.

## Enforcement responsibilities

The maintainer team is responsible for clarifying and enforcing the
standards of acceptable behaviour. They will take appropriate and
fair corrective action in response to any behaviour they deem
inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful, in line with the
Contributor Covenant's enforcement guidelines.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or
reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that
are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate
reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Reporting

Instances of unacceptable behaviour may be reported privately to the
maintainer team at:

- `conduct@huella-latam.undp.org` (placeholder until UNDP confirms
  the canonical address).

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.
The maintainer team is obligated to respect the privacy and security
of the reporter of any incident.

If your report concerns a maintainer, please contact UNDP directly so
that the report can be handled outside the maintainer team.

## Enforcement guidelines

The maintainer team will follow the
**Contributor Covenant Enforcement Guidelines** (Community Impact:
Correction → Warning → Temporary Ban → Permanent Ban) when
determining the consequences of any action they deem in violation of
this Code of Conduct. The full guidelines are documented alongside
the Covenant at the URL above.
